Declaration

Details components are used in the service to provide additional information to users, sometimes linking to practice guidance on GOV.UK.

DRS deviates from the GOV.UK guidelines by collapsing the declaration statement in a details component on the Review your application screen.

After extensive user testing, it was decided to place the statement within a details component.

Documentation (HMLR design system)


User research

After extensive user testing, it was decided to place the statement within a details component.

The decision to have the declaration statement collapsed in a details component was extensively tested. Different methods of ensuring users would read and agree to the statement were tested, including a checkbox.

Testing highlighted an unexpected issue - most users read the declaration when they are trained/first use a service and then never do so again.

Many said they would rather not have to scroll through the long statement each time they submitted an application (which for some users would be ten times per day).

Users stated, if someone is going to break the law, a checkbox is not going to stop them.

Based on this it was decided to collapse the statement into the details component, which has been agreed to by legal and ops outside of the project.
